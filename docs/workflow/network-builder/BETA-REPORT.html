HighHIGH-1

Removing the jump host orphans the exits — canvas says “no network” while the estimate keeps billing a phantom multi-exit network

Reported by: power lens · Files: tool.html — remove() ~850–858, paintCanvas() ~751–765, estimate() ~537–629

Root cause. remove('jump') sets state.jump=null but never touches state.exits. paintCanvas() renders the exits only inside if(jump), so they vanish and the notice switches to “Start by connecting a jump host” — yet estimate()/computePrice() still fold the leftover exits into the total, and the engine’s else-branch emits link edges with from='—' and no entry edge. The canvas, notice, estimate panel and per-edge list all disagree: the user is shown an empty, jump-required board but simultaneously quoted a full priced network for boxes they can no longer see or reach.

Why it matters. It compounds — with no jump you can keep tapping exit palette cards and they silently append (invisible on the canvas), driving the quoted price up while the aria-live region announces the phantom total. This breaks the PRD/TECH state model (edges must be pre-ordered [entry, link×N]; here there is no entry edge yet the breakdown still claims “(jump ×1 + exits)”).

Repro

  1. Open tool.html (default = 1 BWH jump + 2 AaITR exits).
  2. Click the ✕ on the jump slot — or focus the jump slot and press Delete (both paths reproduce).
  3. Canvas collapses to “you → [empty Jump host slot]”, notice reads “Start by connecting a jump host” — but the Estimate panel still shows ① $43.76/mo, ② 137~285 ms, ③ 2,018 capped, ④ 102M, and per-edge rows “— → AaITR → IP 1 / IP 2”.
  4. Now click SolaDrive in the Residential-exits palette (still no jump): it silently appends (invisible), price climbs to $83.76/mo, live region announces “Added SolaDrive as exit 3. Estimated $83.76 per month.”
Fix direction
When the jump is removed, either (a) also clear/park state.exits and zero the estimate, or (b) keep the exits visible as an explicit “disconnected — needs a jump” state that is excluded from the total until a jump is reconnected. Either way the canvas, notice, estimate and edge list must agree, and adding an exit with no jump must not raise the price.

MedMED-1

Metered provider (VirVM) placed as the jump host prices at $0.00/mo and shows “connect a box” although a paid box is placed

Reported by: power lens · Files: tool.html — computePrice(), trafficUI (exit-only render)

Root cause. computePrice() pushes a metered (or reference) jump into references[] and never sums it, and the traffic package/cycle configurator (trafficUI) is rendered only on exit slots — never on the jump slot. So a VirVM jump can never be priced at all: the headline reads “$0.00/mo + ref” and the breakdown reads “connect a box” (the empty-state text) even though VirVM is connected. This is the exact single-relay case a mainland-China user hits (VirVM + 专线GIA as one box) — and they are told it costs $0.00, violating the “never fabricate price / honest-estimate” intent.

Repro

  1. Open tool.html; click VirVM in the Jump-hosts palette (replaces BWH).
  2. Remove the two default AaITR exits (✕ on each).
  3. Estimate panel: ① Price “$0.00/mo + ref”; breakdown text “connect a box”; “reference (not summed): VirVM (jump · 专线GIA metered) (priced as exit — not summed)”.
  4. No traffic-package UI appears on the jump slot, so the price cannot be corrected.
Fix direction
Render the traffic-package + billing-cycle configurator on a metered jump slot too (reuse the exit path), and sum its effective $/mo into the total. A connected paid box must never headline as $0.00.

MedMED-2

The “reference” badge is not a reliable cost signal — DMIT (reference) is excluded from the total, but VMISS (same “reference / out of stock” badge) IS summed

Reported by: power lens + feature-coverage (feature 16 deviation) · Contradicts TECH §1.2 & §1.7

Root cause. Two providers wear the identical “REFERENCE” palette badge and “NOT BENCHMARKED · OUT OF STOCK” slot label, yet behave oppositely in the cost math. DMIT is modelled pricing.type 'reference' → excluded and shown under “reference (not summed)”. VMISS is modelled pricing.type 'flat' (CAD) → its price is summed straight into the total. The badge is derived from the bench flag, not from whether the box counts toward cost, so the honesty signal users rely on (“this box is priced-only, not part of my bill”) is unreliable. TECH §1.2 / §1.7 explicitly list VMISS under the reference type (“no — listed separately, not summed”).

Repro

  1. Open tool.html. Place DMIT as jump → panel shows “reference (not summed): DMIT (jump) (from $10.90)”, DMIT adds $0 (total stays $43.76 from the 2 AaITR exits).
  2. Reload and place VMISS as jump → breakdown line “VMISS (shared jump) — $4.38 (CA$6)” is summed into total $48.14/mo — while the VMISS card is badged “REFERENCE” and the slot reads “CN2 GIA · NOT BENCHMARKED · OUT OF STOCK”.
Fix direction (PM decision)
Either (a) drive the badge from the cost-inclusion flag, not the bench flag, so a summed box never wears “reference”; or (b) reclassify VMISS as reference per TECH §1.2 and list it not-summed. Coverage tester notes VMISS has exact list prices (not a “from $X” range), so a summed model is defensible — but then the badge must change. PM to pick.

1.4 · Low-severity & notes

LowLOW-1

Price-breakdown label “= $X/mo (jump ×1 + exits)” is inaccurate when the jump contributes $0 or is absent

Reported by: power lens · Reinforces HIGH-1 confusion

The multi-line breakdown footer always reads “= $X/mo (jump ×1 + exits)” whenever more than one line item exists. With a reference jump (DMIT) or metered jump (VirVM) the jump contributes $0, so “jump ×1” overstates what is counted; in the orphaned-exits state (no jump) the same text appears with no jump present at all. Cosmetic/labeling, but it reinforces the phantom-jump confusion of HIGH-1.

Repro — place DMIT (or VirVM) as jump with exits present: “= $43.76/mo (jump ×1 + exits)” though the jump added $0. Or trigger the orphan state (remove jump with exits present): still “(jump ×1 + exits)”. See 02_removejump_exits_orphaned.png (above).

The China guardrail toggle is a visually-hidden native <input id=nbx-toggle> inside label.switch. Playwright element-level clicks on the input itself fail (“not visible”) — worth a quick confirmation that the label remains the intended, sized hit target. Testability nit, not a user-facing defect.

3Verdict & readiness

Verdict  Conditional pass — functionally complete, one HIGH bug blocks Gate 3.

All 21 features PASS and every ground-truth checkpoint (default $73.76 board, China guardrail, metered/currency math, 8-exit fan-out, the choose.html Value column) reproduced exactly, with no horizontal overflow 320–1440 and a11y holding. But the builder is not yet ready for Gate 3: removing the jump host leaves a phantom, invisible network that is still billed (HIGH-1), and two MEDIUM honesty defects remain — a metered jump prices at $0.00 (MED-1) and the “reference” badge is an unreliable cost signal (MED-2).

Recommendation: fix HIGH-1 and MED-1, take a PM decision on MED-2 (badge vs model), then re-run the beta pass on those edge sequences before Gate 3. LOW-1/LOW-2 are cosmetic/testability and can ride along.
